As a businesswoman, I care deeply about my customers. But like anyone for whom you feel affection, customers can also drive you mad. They will come rushing in, saying their handbag has been stolen. They will swear that they left it in the changing room, create havoe (混乱) and then find it had been in their car all the time. They will have out half the goods in the shop, and want the only style you do not have left in a particular colour. I do know how upset the shop staff can get, but I try to persuade them to keep smiling.
  作为一名女商人，我非常关心我的顾客。但是，就像你所爱的人一样，顾客也会让你发疯。他们会冲进来说他们的手提包被偷了。他们会发誓把它放在更衣室里，制造出混乱，然后发现它一直在他们的车里。他们会把店里一半的商品翻出来，只想要你没有的那种自然色。我知道商店的工作人员会有多难过，但我试图说服他们保持微笑。
  I remember the first really difficult customer we had at Covent Garden. She was displeased with absolutely everything, nothing was right and I was rather surpised that she became a “regular”. After a while, she apologied for the way she behaved at the beginning. She had split up with her husband the week before, was living in a flat by herself, and since she had found it too much to cope with (应对), she had taken it out on other people.
  我记得我们在考文特花园遇到的第一个非常难对付的顾客。她对一切都不满意，什么都不对，我很惊讶她成了“正常的人”。过了一段时间，她为自己一开始的行为道歉。她前一周和丈夫分手了，一个人住在一套公寓里，由于她觉得太难应付了，她就把这事发泄到别人身上。

  That taught me a valuable lesson and I pass it on to the people who work in the market. Do not take it pesonally. If a customer is rude or difficult, just think “Maybe she has had a row with her husband. Maybe her child is not well.” Always water it down and do not let your ego (自我) get in the way. If you do, you will not be able to handle it and the whole thing develops into an unpleasant scene and that ruins everyone’s day.
  这给了我一个宝贵的教训，我把它传给了在市场工作的人。别把它当真。如果一个人粗鲁或者很难对付，只是想想“她和她丈夫吵架了，也许她的孩子身体不太好。”一定要把不爽的感觉忍下去，不要让你的自尊心挡在路上（从而使内心的不爽爆发出来）。如果你这样做了，你将无法处理这件事，这个整个事情会发展成一个不愉快的场景，并且会破坏每个人的一天。
