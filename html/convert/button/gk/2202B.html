   In 1916, two girls of wealthy families, best friends from Auburn, N. Y.—Dorothy Woodruff and Rosamond Underwood—traveled to a settlement in the Rocky Mountains to teach in a one-room schoolhouse. The girls had gone to Smith College. They wore expensive clothes. So for them to move to Elkhead, Colo. to instruct the children whose shoes were held together with string was a surprise. Their stay in Elkhead is the subject of Nothing Daunted: The Unexpected Education of Two Society Girls in the West by Dorothy Wickenden, who is a magazine editor and Dorothy Woodruff's granddaughter.
1916年，两位来自纽约奥本的富有家庭的女孩——多萝西·伍德拉夫和罗莎芒德·安德伍德，她们是同学也是最好的朋友， 到落基山脉的一个定居点去教一间小学。这两个女孩毕业于史密斯学院，穿着昂贵的衣服。所以她们要搬到科罗拉 多州的埃尔克黑德为那些用绳子缚着鞋的孩子上课，这着实让人吃惊。她们在埃尔克黑德的经历是《未见曾有： 两位社交名媛在西部的意外教育》的主题，作者是多萝西·威肯登，她是杂志编辑，也是多萝西·伍德拉夫的孙女。

   Why did they go then? Well, they wanted to do something useful. Soon, however, they realized what they had undertaken.
那么，她们为什么会去呢？哦，她们想要做一些有用的事情。然而，很快她们就意识到了自己所承担的任务。
   They moved in with a local family, the Harrisons, and, like them, had little privacy, rare baths, and a blanket of snow on their quilt when they woke up in the morning. Some mornings, Rosamond and Dorothy would arrive at the schoolhouse to find the children weeping from the cold. In spring, the snow was replaced by mud over ice.
她们搬进了当地的哈里森一家人的家中，和他们一样，生活几乎没有什么隐私，洗澡的机会也很少， 当她们早上醒来时，被冰雪覆盖的棉被让她们很不适应。有些早晨，罗莎芒德和多萝西到达学校教书时， 会发现孩子们因寒冷而哭泣。到了春天，积雪被冻土替换。
   In Wickenden's book, she expanded on the history of the West and also on feminism, which of course influenced the girls' decision to go to Elkhead. A hair-raising section concerns the building of the railroads, which entailed（牵涉）drilling through the Rockies, often in blinding snowstorms. The book ends with Rosamond and Dorothy's return to Auburn.
在威肯登的书中，她对西部的历史和女权主义进行了扩展，这当然影响了女孩们去埃尔克黑德的决定。书中还 包括了一个让人毛骨悚然的章节，涉及修建铁路的故事，这通常需要在刺眼的暴风雪中穿越落基山脉。书的结尾 是罗莎芒德和多萝西回到奥本。
   Wickenden is a very good storyteller. The sweep of the land and the stoicism（坚忍）of the people move her to some beautiful writing. Here is a picture of Dorothy Woodruff, on her horse, looking down from a hill top: "When the sun slipped behind the mountains, it shed a rosy glow all around them. Then a full moon rose. The snow was marked only by small animals: foxes, coyotes, mice, and varying hares, which turned white in the winter."
威肯登是一个非常好的文学故事讲述者。广阔的土地和人民的坚忍性格，启发了她一些美丽的文字。以下是 多萝西·伍德拉夫骑马站在山顶上观望的一幅画面：“当太阳滑落在山后，它在它们周围洒下了玫瑰色的光芒。然后， 满月升起。只有小动物的踪迹在雪地上呈现：狐狸、土狼、老鼠和变色鸟，它们在冬天变成白色。”
