Swap. Do not Shop!
换物不买新！
  You keep hearing about recycling, right? But it doesn’t end with bottles, cans, and paper. Clothing takes a huge amount of natural resources（资源）to make, and buying loads of new clothing (or throwing out old clothing) is not healthy for the environment. So what to do with all those perfectly-goodbut-you’re-maybe-a-little-sick-of-them clothes piled on your bedroom flfloor? Hold a clothing swap. It’s the best way to get rid of your used clothes, score clothes from your friends, and have a party all at the same time.

  你一直在听说回收利用，对吧？但这并不仅限于瓶子、罐子和纸张。制作服装需要大量的自然资源，而购买大量新衣服（或扔掉旧衣服）对环境也不健康。那么，应该怎么处理堆积成山的旧衣服呢？可以举行一次换衣聚会。这既可以处理掉过去的衣服，又可以从朋友那里得到新衣服，还可以一边聚会一边欢度时光。
  A successful swap depends on the selection of clothes, the organization of the event, and, obviously, how much fun is had. It is really easy to do! Here are a few pointers.

  一个成功的换衣聚会取决于衣服的选择，事件的组织，以及显然与会者的欢乐程度。其实这非常简单！以下是几个要点：
  Invite 5-10 people so you have a nice selection. Less people than that, and there may not be enough things to choose from; more than that, and it becomes uncontrollable.

  邀请5-10人参加，这样你就可以获得很好的选择范围。不到这个人数，可能无法提供足够的选择；超过这个人数，会失去控制。
  Tell everyone to bring clean clothes in good condition. They should also prepare plenty of reusable bags to carry their“new”clothes home.

  让每个人带干净且状况良好的衣服参加派对。他们也应该准备足够的可重复使用的袋子来携带他们的“新”衣服回家。
  Put different types of clothing on different surfaces in the room. Have everyone put their clothes in the right spots. Place a few mirrors around your room so people can see how things look when they try them on. One of the ground rules of the swap should be that everyone must try on the clothes before they take them—things always look different when you put them on.

  房间中的不同表面上放置不同类型的衣服。让每个人将自己的衣服放在合适的位置。在房间周围摆放一些镜子，使人们试穿时可以看到自己的样子。聚会的一个底线规则是，在拿走衣服之前，每个人都必须试穿——因为当你穿上衣服时，它们总是会有些不同。
  Set a starting time. Maybe you say“go”, or turn on a certain song, or whatever. Just keep music playing throughout. And don’t forget to put out some cookies and fruits. Remember, it’s a party!

  设定一个开始时间。可以说“开始”，或播放某个特定的歌曲，或采用其他方法。只要始终保持音乐播放即可。不要忘记准备一些饼干和水果。记住，这是一场聚会！
